Project Ideas


Overview


My project will be developing a smartphone app for delivering takeaway food and drink around Australia named Clytie. Although there are many food delivery apps operated in Australia, this app will be the collaboration among those food apps such as Foodora, UberEats, Deliveroo and Menulogs. Besides, the app also shows how many kcal of any food or drink before purchasing in order to balance the energy and control the weight. Especially, this Clytie app also allows user to pay by cash on delivery or card after ordering. This smartphone app is promised to be designed with easy-to-view interface.


Motivation


According to Carey (2018), Australians are spending an incredible $2.6 billion each year on food and drink delivery through companies such as Menulog, UberEats, Deliveroo and Foodora (Figure 1). Additionally, Friday and Saturday nights are the most popular ordering times (Sullivan 2017). In other words, developing this app will take advantage of creating network with delivery service companies like Menulog, UberEats, Deliveroo and Foodora, and making a huge profits from Australia citizens.


Description


To illustrate, this Clytie app will be suitable for all ages and be charged $10 AUD when it is downloaded and installed on any users' smartphone. The reason why this app is surcharged whereas other apps like Menulog, UberEats, Deliveroo and Foodora are free installation is that it guarantees a 5-minute delivery earlier even in rush hours.
So how to use it next? Consumer needs to sign up an user account in order to save the delivery address and accumulate points for discount program at the end of each year. After that, the app will automatically log in that account. Of course the ordering style of Clytie app is as same as other apps such as Menulog, UberEats, Deliveroo and Foodora. It will auto access the current location of that account to find and suggest which restaurants are nearby as well as recommend the best-seller foods and drinks in those restaurants. Or otherwise, user can search any restaurants' name or favourite food which they like in the ‘search' box. Also this app will show which partners in charge of that restaurant to facilitate the bill and the deliverer. Then the app will automatically send message back to user to confirm the order after they have chosen food or drink from restaurant as well as the estimated time. Especially, the difference of this app is that user can choose one of two payment ways. One is paying cash when receiving order, and another is paying immediately by credit card when order is confirmed. Besides, user can also keep track the process as this smartphone app will send message after each done step. Please see picture to take a look more detail and clearer.

Before creating this Clytie app, I have to negotiate and corporate with other partners in Australia such as Menulog, UberEats, Deliveroo and Foodora in order to get access to maps, restaurants’ name, restaurants’ menu, menu price, estimated time to do the order, and so on from these partners. Therefore, one of the biggest problems here is to get permission from those partners as each company want to keep confidential contact of restaurants in order to get its own profit and revenue in the monopoly way. Another issue is structuring the financial cost for myself and company partners. In partners’ side, I think of about providing and developing the advanced software. Towards me, the revenue will be harvested from the installation fee minus the Google and Apple fees because both Google and Apple take 30 per cent of sales once your app is published (Franco 2018). One more thing is that menu of restaurant may change any time so this app needs to be updated at the same time with this situation.
And how long will Clytie app be launched? In my opinion, building this app is easy and in a shorter time, about three to four months but cooperating with other partner company may be a little difficult, about one to two years. However, I still believe in this app.
